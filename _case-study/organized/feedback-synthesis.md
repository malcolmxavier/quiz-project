# Customer Feedback Synthesis — January through April

## The Headline

**"Fine but forgettable."** That exact phrase appears in three separate months of feedback. It's the program's problem in three words.

The gap between Basecamp's brand (NPS 67 — strong) and Basecamp Rewards (NPS 12 — weak) is the real story.

---

## Satisfaction Trend (Declining)

| Month | Very Sat + Sat | Neutral | Dissatisfied |
|-------|---------------|---------|--------------|
| Jan   | 46%           | 41%     | 13%          |
| Feb   | 41%           | 44%     | 15%          |
| Mar   | 37%           | 47%     | 16%          |
| Apr   | 34%           | 48%     | 18%          |

Positive sentiment is slowly shrinking. Neutrals are the biggest group every month — that's the "forgettable" majority.

---

## Recurring Themes

### 1. Forgettable & Invisible
- "I forget about it mostly"
- "Honestly forgot I was a member until you emailed me"
- "I have the app somewhere on my phone"
- "basecamp coffee has a rewards program? news to me"

### 2. No Brand Personality
- "This program has no personality. It's just points."
- "Generic. Could be any coffee chain."
- "The program doesn't feel like Basecamp. It feels like corporate stuff."
- "I wish this felt more like Basecamp and less like every other chain"
- **The killer line:** *"Your coffee has personality. Your rewards program doesn't. Fix that and you'd have something."*

### 3. Transactional, Not Relational
- "I don't feel like a valued customer, I feel like a transaction"
- "Nothing makes me feel special as a member"
- "I've been coming here 2 years but the app treats me like a stranger"
- "What's the point of points if there's nothing fun about it?"

### 4. Tier System Isn't Working
- "I don't understand the tiers"
- "What's an Explorer? Am I supposed to care?"
- "I've been coming here for a year and I'm still a Trailblazer. What does that even mean?"
- Baristas can't explain the tiers either.

### 5. Losing to Competitors on Experience
- "I go to Starbucks when I want rewards, here when I want good coffee"
- "Starbucks at least has games. Dutch Bros remembers my name."
- Starbucks wins with gamification; Dutch Bros wins with personal relationships; Basecamp is stuck in the middle.

### 6. Promos Bump but Don't Stick
- Valentine's double points → 23% lift, gone the next day.
- No sustained engagement from tactical promos.

---

## What Customers Seem to WANT (Hidden in the Feedback)

- **Help finding their drink**: *"I wish you could help me find new drinks to try."* / *"What if you helped people find their signature drink?"*
- **To be known**: *"People light up when we ask about their coffee preferences, but the app doesn't capture any of that."*
- **Something that feels like Basecamp**: personality, not points.

---

## App Engagement Snapshot (March)
- 847 app opens / avg session 23 seconds
- Most common action: checking point balance (transactional)
- Only 12 rewards redeemed

Short, shallow, transactional — no reason to linger.

---

## The Diagnosis

Customers love Basecamp's coffee and vibe. They don't dislike the program — they just don't feel it. It's generic, it doesn't know them, and it doesn't reflect the brand. The problem isn't the rewards; it's the lack of identity and personalization.

**That "coffee personality" idea from Marcus's notes is starting to look less random.**
